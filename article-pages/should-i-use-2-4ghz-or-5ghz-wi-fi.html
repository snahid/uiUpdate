Should I use 2.4GHz or 5GHz Wi-Fi

There are so many elements that come with picking out the right Wi-Fi for your home and your specific needs, such as what GHz to use. If you are stuck between getting a 2.4GHz or a 5GHz, understand that these numbers are referring to different "bands" that your Wi-Fi can use for its signal and speed. The 2.4GHz option supports up to 450 Mbps or 600 Mbps, depending on the router class, whereas 5GHz will support up to 1300 Mbps. Still unsure what to choose? Here is a closer look at the two Wi-Fi options and how to determine which would be the best fit for you.

2.4GHz Frequency

If you are trying to cover a significant distance, say on a college campus or at a park, 2.4GHz is the frequency you want to use. As opposed to 5GHz, 2.4GHz can reach greater distances, penetrate obstructions easier, and cost less money because you need fewer repeaters for coverage. Just about all devices today support 2.4GHz frequencies, so compatibility is a non-issue.

One disadvantage of 2.4GHz is that many types of technologies use this frequency range, such as cordless phones, microwaves, Bluetooth devices, etc. These devices can interfere with Wi-Fi connections, meaning you may get dead spots and weakened areas of service. If you are outdoors, this is generally not something you will have to worry about. However, in an apartment complex or a sizeable multi-story building, it can be a problem.

5GHz Frequency

As opposed to the 2.4GHz frequency, 5GHz has a far higher threshold for speed and efficiency. While it does suffer in range, it is ideal for fast file transfer speeds and reliable connectivity. Generally, with 5GHz, the roll off of signal is far quicker than 2.4GHz, so you will know rather quickly where the signal will not reach. Since 5GHz is less used with the most common devices, you will have a reduced risk of interference as compared to 2.4GHz.

On the downside, 5GHz does not penetrate obstructions as well as 2.4GHz, so this can be a problem if you are in a cluttered area with many walls and floors.

How Do I Know Which One Will Work Better for Me?

The first question to answer is, how fast do you need your connection to be? If you are going to be transferring large files or streaming heavy loads of media over Wi-Fi, 5GHz may be the more desirable option. For more confidence, feel free to stress test both the 2.4GHz and 5GHz options to see which one performs better for you. Walk around with your cell phone and see where the signals reach for each one. If you find that you will need more repeaters with 5GHz than 2.4GHz, the cost will be higher, and that may deter you from going with that option.

Conclusion

As you can see, going with 2.4GHz or 5GHz is entirely up to your personal situation. Both can provide you excellent Wi-Fi usage for their respected strong points. By using the tips above, you should be able to better determine what you need based on your desires and where you live. Take the time to evaluate so you can make the right call and be certain you choose the right one.
