Wi-Fi In An Emergency – Can It Help?

With the emergence of more and more public Wi-Fi networks and hotspots, people are spending less on traditional connection costs such as airtime and mobile data. Also, in South Africa, our mobile data costs are still quite high compared to the rest of the world. But if you find yourself in an emergency where you’ve run out of data and airtime, you don’t necessarily have to panic. Not if you have access to a Wi-Fi network. Here are a few example scenarios where access to Wi-Fi can come in handy if you have no other way of connecting.

If you’re lost and need to find your way to safety

Say you’re on your way to work and want to avoid traffic, but then end up in an unfamiliar area and your data is all out. Say, you’re out on a delivery but run out of data mid-way through your trip and now have no idea where you are. Getting lost can be very stressful but, if there’s a nearby Wi-Fi network, you could get yourself out of trouble. In order to access a GPS system, you need to be connected to the internet. So, if you can find a nearby shopping centre, petrol station or restaurant, you can connect to Wi-Fi and navigate your way home. If that’s too complicated, you can also always use a calling app like WhatsApp or Skype to ask someone to come and get you. Problem solved.

If you’re out of cash and need to make a payment

How often do you leave the house thinking you have cash in your wallet, but then get to a shop or restaurant having forgotten about the money you spent the day before? The cash you thought you had isn’t there. If establishments will allow it, and if you have access to Wi-Fi, you can always do an EFT or instant money transfer.

Another common scenario is that a shop or restaurant has a cash-only policy, so you can’t pay with a card or EFT. These days, banking apps offer clever solutions to this where you can send yourself a voucher using your app and withdraw cash from a nearby ATM. However, this requires access to an internet connection. If, again, you’re out of mobile data, Wi-Fi can help. You need never be too strapped for cash.

When you’re travelling

Because international sim cards and mobile data fees tend to be very pricey, using public Wi-Fi when you travel is a cost-effective solution to communication. But it means that when you’re out of a Wi-Fi zone, you’re a bit vulnerable. Fortunately, most common areas like coffee shops, hotels and malls – especially in popular tourist countries – offer open Wi-Fi networks. This makes it easier for you to communicate with people back home, find your way using GPS, and research interesting places to see on your travels. Often when we’re travelling, our normal mobile data is out of the question, so Wi-Fi becomes the solution.

Conclusion

It isn’t advised to let your airtime or mobile data run completely dry, because when you’re in a jam it really helps to just be able to call someone. But, if you find yourself in a sticky situation, try to find an open Wi-Fi network and you’ll soon be able to sort yourself out. In these types of emergencies, Wi-Fi is an easy and convenient solution.
