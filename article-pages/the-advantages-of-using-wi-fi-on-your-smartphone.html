The Advantages of Using Wi-Fi on Your Smartphone

If you have a smartphone, then chances are you have used Wi-Fi at some point or another. Brands like Apple or Motorola Droid allow people to access and perform online takes on the web right from their phones. In fact, internet access is deemed the most important feature people look for when purchasing a smartphone. Aside from being able to surf the web, here are some other advantages of using Wi-Fi on your smartphone that you may not be aware of.

Save on Data Usage

Most people do not have unlimited data plans, and even if you do, there are limits built into the contract. Companies like AT&T slow your speed dramatically after using 22.5Gb of data. However, using Wi-Fi significantly helps keep your data usage. If you are on a pay as you go plan where you pay by the gigabyte or megabyte, it is essential that you use Wi-Fi to avoid charges.

Save On Battery Life

t takes far more battery life to use cellular service than your local Wi-Fi. This is especially true if you are in an area with weak cell phone service, your phone will constantly be reaching out for a signal which requires far more battery than if you had a strong signal. With Wi-Fi, the power requirements are far less of a strain on your battery. This not only keeps your current charge lasting longer, but is also good for the longevity of the entire life of your battery because you do not have to recharge it as often, which wears it out.

Google Fi

Services like Google Fi live on Wi-Fi connectivity to support its very low pricing model. Google Fi uses Sprint, T-Mobile, and US Cellular services to support its 4G/LTE data network and charges $10/GB of data. Monthly costs are a fraction of larger carriers like AT&T and Verizon, and it is all made possible by using Wi-Fi rather than cellular service. To keep your bill low, utilize every free public Wi-Fi hotspot you can find.

Increased Speed

If you are in an area with older cellular speeds or in low service areas, your cellular data speeds will be next to nothing compared to home or business Wi-Fi. Theoretical 4G/LTE speeds on a good signal can range from 50-100mbps symmetrical, but home Wi-Fi on cable internet can have speeds ranging from 50mbps to 1,000mpbs, and the signal will be far more substantial and more reliable. So not only are you saving on battery and data usage, but your overall download/upload speeds will be lightyears faster than if you stayed on the cellular networks.

Conclusion

As you can see, Wi-Fi is a powerful resource that does more than just connect you to the internet through your smartphone. It holds immense capabilities that you can take advantage of that will increase the quality of your phone as well as your overall experiences. Connect to Wi-Fi often with your smartphone to gain the benefits it as to offer.
