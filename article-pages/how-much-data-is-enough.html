How Much Data Is Enough?

There are so many options for data packages at the moment that it’s hard to know which one is best for you. And, with more public Wi-Fi networks becoming available, it doesn’t make sense to spend too much money on mobile data if it might go to waste. If you’re not sure how many GB you need, here’s a basic breakdown of what the different options will get you. And, depending on what you’re doing, how many GB will keep you up to date and efficient.

For an individual

It depends on several things – how often you surf the net, if you work from home, if you download movies etc. Generally speaking, if you spend a few hours a day on the internet, use apps like Facebook and WhatsApp to chat to friends and family, stream music or movies once in a while and have the occasional Skype call, 10GB should be enough.

If you’re what’s considered a ‘frequent user’ – someone who loves browsing the web, downloading apps, streaming music and movies and spends up to 80 hours on Skype – then, you’re looking at a different requirement. A good 25GB should cover you. And, if you’re a professional who works from home, e-mails large files and needs internet access to do their work, it’s more likely to be 50GB.

For a home

Depending on how many family members there are, and how many of them need daily access to the internet, around 50GB should be enough. This is usually sufficient for thousands of hours of internet browsing, hundreds of hours of music streaming and allows one person to occasionally work from home. However, if there are several teenagers in the house, or if both parents work from home a few times a week, this number might be too low. Contact your service provider for a more unique breakdown.

For a business

This is also a tricky one to estimate. Obviously, the bigger the business, the more data you’ll need. And large corporate companies with hundreds of staff members will probably seek alternative deals. However, if you run a small business from an office or home, and need to connect up to 3 computers to use purely for work, you’re looking at around 100GB per month. This amount of data offers good connectivity, allows you to send thousands of e-mails and large documents, and also offers loads of room for streaming, downloads and video calls or meetings on apps like Skype or Zoom. This is considered ‘heavy usage’. But, as your business grows, you’ll have to re-look at how much data you’ll need. Nothing is more frustrating than trying to conduct business in a professional environment with a slow or unreliable internet connection.

Conclusion

It’s always difficult to predict exactly how much data you’ll need every month, as things can change. This is an estimated and rough breakdown but, if you’re still not sure what will be enough for you, contact your service provider for extra advice. You can never be too informed.
