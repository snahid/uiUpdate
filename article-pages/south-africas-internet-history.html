South Africa’s Internet History

South Africa and its history with the internet is quite remarkable. Even though the internet is still "young" in an overview sense, it has a profound impact on the world. In just a few short decades, it has become one of the most interesting historical chapters and is worth reminiscing over.

The Beginnings

Starting out in the beginning stages, the first use of the internet in South Africa can be traced back to 1988 at Rhodes University. Rhodes University is a public research college that is located in Grahamstown in Eastern Cape Providence, South Africa. It is also one of the four universities in that area.

In 1988, There are three incredible individuals named Francois Guilarmod, Mike Lawrie, and Dave Wilson, who established an internet link using email. The purpose was to exchange an email between the Control Data Cyber computer at the university and what was called Fidonet gateway in Portland, Oregon. For further clarification, Fidonet is a computer network that is used for communicating between bulletin board systems.

The 1990’s

Though it was just one email at that moment in time, it was the stepping stone that launched a rapid wave for the internet to begin growing and thriving in South Africa. Just one short year later, a dial-up internet system was implemented, which gave even more access and opportunities. Then, fast forwarding to 1991, South Africa had a full internet connection that operated over a leased line. Even as great as this was, most of this internet development occurred within academic institutions, and it was not until a bit later that it was pushed to the broader commercial world. Once the public, small business owners and corporate leaders saw what the internet was capable of, the race was on.

South Africa's first commercial ISP (Internet Service Provider) was The Internetworking Company, which was established in November of 1993. Nearly a dozen commercial companies had access to the internet within the first month and began widening internet availability throughout the nation. One of these early customers was TIS (The Internet Solution), which was an internet provider that commercialized and broadened South Africa's access to the internet.

Heading into the year 1997, 56kpbs dial-up connections began to become more present, and products like MWEB's Big Black Box brought on many new subscribers. The rise in internet usage across South Africa was exponential right through 2002 when the company Telkom launch its first ASDL product, offering download speeds of 512kbps. 3G and Wi-Fi technologies quickly came onto the scene in 2004 with Sentech MyWireless and Vodacom 3G, offering download speeds up to 1Mbps.

Today

From that first email to where we are today, it is quite fascinating to see how quickly the internet grew. Currently, South Africans enjoy broadband speeds that exceed 100Mbps, which keeps up with the rest of the developed world. Services such as Cable, DSL, and Satellite internet are now ubiquitous, the prices per megabit are more affordable than ever, and millions of people across South Africa have internet access to experience.
