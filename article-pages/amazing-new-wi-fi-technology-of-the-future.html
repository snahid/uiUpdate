Amazing New Wi-Fi Technology of the Future

Wi-Fi was first introduced in 1997, and since then the technology has grown significantly to become where it is today. It allows people not to be stuck in one particular spot and gives more freedom than ever before to access the internet from anywhere, especially if you have a smartphone. Though it has come a long way, there is always room for improvement, especially with the demand continuing to rise for Wi-Fi connection. With this mind, future improved Wi-Fi technologies are being designed and will be implemented in the near future. The hope is to remediate current issues and be a stepping stone for even more enhancements later on.

6Ghz Wi-Fi SuperFi-Way

From Wireless A/B to AC3200 with multiband technology, 6Ghz Wi-Fi SuperFi-Way brings faster speeds for more devices than ever before. This next big push for SuperFi-Way will start hitting the market hard in 2021, which includes dependable 1Gpbs speeds and averages at a 300 percent improvement in user density.

How This Benefits You

With SuperFi-Way delivering many advantages in density versus previous Wi-Fi generations, you might be wondering how this new spectrum benefits you. If you have numerous technologies and devices in your home that use 2.4 and 5Ghz frequencies, then you may be experiencing interference collisions between devices. By using the 6Ghz frequency, you will be effectively eliminating this interference problem.

Furthermore, with an entirely new wireless spectrum and a 300 percent increase in user density, you can have more devices connected that can pull harder on your Wi-Fi connection than previously. This allows the use of more high definition security cameras, uncompressed high-quality video streaming, and latency sensitive gaming devices without interruption or dropped connections.

Who Else Benefits?

Companies like Amazon and UPS could benefit from this technology as well. Most freight transports via trucking and railroad, home deliveries make some portion of their journey by flight. The 5 GHz Wi-Fi spectrum is generally prohibited at airports and automatically disabled by both APs and clients to avoid interference with air traffic radar systems. All buildings within a distance of an airport have a limited 5 GHz spectrum for the same reason. The 6GHz frequency spectrum does not have this restriction, and it can resolve these conflicts while providing considerable improvements in performance for this sector of the economy.

Another one of the new technologies that will take advantage of this new Wi-Fi technology is Virtual Reality Learning in the healthcare industry. These virtual reality sessions require large swaths of wireless throughput to deliver the VR world. A classroom full of students with the most common Wi-Fi technologies would not be able to stream the high definition video over a Wi-Fi connection to their headsets without running into issues.

Conclusion

Wi-Fi technology has come a long way in the last 20 years. With more and more devices being connected to the internet through Wi-Fi, it only stands to reason that the technology needs to keep up with the demand. With that motive, these new upgrades and advantages are certainly something to look forward to for better connection, speed, and a much better overall experience for users.
