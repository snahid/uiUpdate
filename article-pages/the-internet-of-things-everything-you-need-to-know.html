The Internet of Things – Everything You Need To Know?

For a few years now, we’ve been hearing about how our world is becoming more and more inter-connected. From Alexa playing music requests on demand to smart toasters and home automation. We know that the ‘Internet of Things’ (IoT) helps bring networks together, but what does that mean for us? And, what can we expect from IoT in the future?

What is IoT?

The term might sound unusual but, at its most basic, it refers to any machines, devices, smartphones, cameras etc that are connected to the internet. According to tech expert and IoT specialist, Matthew Evans, the Internet of Things can be explained like this: “Simply, the Internet of Things is made up of devices – from simple sensors to smartphones and wearables – connected together”. It allows devices to ‘communicate’ in some form and has become a prominent solution in the collection and analysis of data.

How is IoT used?

There are myriad application opportunities for the ‘Internet of Things’ and, since the term was first coined in 1999, its popularity has only increased. We’re used to using things like smart watches, virtual assistants like Alexa and Siri, but what are some of the other applications of IoT?

Here are a few of the ways IoT is being used today…

  • In the home
    The options are almost endless but, admittedly, South Africa is a bit behind on the domestic technology uptake. However, some of the products on offer globally include smart mirrors, robot vacuum cleaners, smart lighting, wireless appliances, intelligent heating, and automated windows and doors. With all these innovative products available, why wouldn’t you sign up for domestic technology? Before you get too excited, know that there have been questions around privacy and security, as well as whether this technology really makes our lives better or easier.

  • In business In many businesses, the implementation of IoT has made operations more efficient, more cost-effective, and has helped cut down on emissions. It also helps businesses streamline production lines and accurately analyse their operations. Companies in industries such as manufacturing, healthcare and even agriculture use connected equipment to help them run effectively. IoT is also used to gain insights, analyse customer experiences, and help reduce waste and increase productivity.

Are there safety and privacy concerns?

Unfortunately, yes. Because anything that’s connected to the internet has the potential to be hacked. So, the information and data stored on these inter-connected devices could be at risk. There’s also the question of privacy, because who knows where this data is being sent and how it is being used. Initially, there were major concerns about the security of IoT but, as the technology and security systems evolve, we’re assured that everything is becoming safer to use.

Where to next?

It’s safe to say that we’ll be seeing more and more applications of IoT in the next few years, from efficient home and business innovations to developments in the robotic and Artificial Intelligence spaces. We first thought this technology would be a thing of the future, but it is increasingly becoming part of our present.
