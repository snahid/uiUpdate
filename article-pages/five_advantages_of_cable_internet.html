5 Advantages of Cable Internet

Cable Internet is a broadband connection that utilizes cable lines, or even cable television networks to transmit data. It requires a cable modem and provides a faster internet connection that is affordable. The most basic option you will find is cable speeds carry data from 6 Mbps to 18 Mbps. For some more insight, here are some advantages you can expect to gain from investing in cable internet.

Faster Speeds

Cable internet operates over the coaxial wire, which offers a much higher ceiling of internet bandwidth. Unlike DSL, Dial-up, or Satellite internet, the cable can reach download speeds up to 1,000Mpbs. DSL and Satellite generally fall between 3mpbs-15mpbs and Dial-up just 0.056mbps. Speed is immensely crucial if you want to stream high definition TV and play games without buffering or timing out.

Lower Latency

Latency is how fast it takes for the data from your computer to reach a service like Google or Call of Duty game servers and back again. This is especially important for gaming online with friends because you want everyone who is playing to be in sync with one another. If someone pulls the trigger of their controller and it takes 1-2 seconds for the game servers and your computer to catch up, the game will be unplayable. 1-2 seconds may not seem like a long time, but in gaming, it is everything.

More People Can Use It at Once

One of the most significant advantages of cable internet is the upload speed. This is important when you have a family and dozens of devices reaching out to the internet at the same time. Cable interest offers upload speeds generally in the 5-10mpbs range, which is plenty for a family to operate on. Dial-up only offers 0.768mbps upload and is barely good enough for one person or device, let alone two.

More Potential for Speed Increase Over Time

Unlike DSL, Dial-up, and Satellite, Cable internet has the potential to increase its speed because the infrastructure can handle it. Since DSL infrastructure is generally old and outdated, you will not ever see a DSL connection be upgraded from 10mbps to 500mpbs. If you have a 100mbps cable connection, it could theoretically be upgraded to 1,000mpbs without the need for enhanced infrastructure.

Cheaper for What You Get

When you compare how much bandwidth you get with cable versus DSL, dollar for dollar, cable wins every time. Many DSL companies charge around $40 for a 3-7mbps connection, and cable companies generally offer 100mbps for around $80. Per megabyte, you are paying far less for cable internet than you are for DSL. Even if you do not need all of that speed, it seems only reasonable that you buy a slower package from a cable provider and have the potential to upgrade if you need it.

Conclusion

It is no secret that cable internet is widely used for its advantages it provides, both cost and performance. This option is reliable and consistent, which is why it is so popular amongst households and businesses. As technology continues to thrive, cable internet lines have gone through tons of advancements and developments that make it an excellent option for people today.
