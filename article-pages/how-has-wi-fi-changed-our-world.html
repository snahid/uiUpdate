How Has Wi-Fi Changed Our World?

In our technologically-advanced era, it’s almost impossible to imagine a time when we didn’t have access to Wi-Fi. Or, more broadly, the internet. These new technologies have made individuals and societies more efficient, more connected and, in some cases, have even helped to save money. We can do almost anything at the touch of a button and information is at our fingertips. Do we even remember what life without the internet was like? And, how have things like Wi-Fi and the internet changed the world?

The way we socialise

Does anyone remember writing a letter to a family member or pen pal? Or, setting a specific time to chat to a friend and heading down to the local phone booth to make the call? What about getting out the Yellow Pages? Not so long ago, this was how people communicated. Now, keeping in contact with friends and family is as easy as opening an app and instantly sending a message, or dialling any number anywhere in the world and chatting on Skype. You can even see one another even though you might be miles apart. With access to Wi-Fi and the internet, international friendships are easier to maintain and online dating is a social norm. No longer do we have to wait days for the post to arrive just to see how our mate is doing.

The way we work

Before e-mail and instant messaging, colleagues sent faxes or letters. In order to share documents, they had to be printed, packed and couriered to your business partner across town. Now, files are simply shared as an attachment in a quick e-mail.

Not only has Wi-Fi changed the way we conduct day-to-day work, but also how we work. Essentially, it can be from anywhere in the world. You don’t need to be in the same office to have a meeting with your boss and teams can work from home and still deliver top-quality goods. This technology has given rise to a generation of remote workers and freelance content producers who are taking advantage of new developments in order to do their job from wherever they are.

The way we travel

Remember using a map to get from one point to another? Or, not so long ago, using navigation systems like Garmin or TomTom? Before you left home, you had to map out a specific route to someone’s physical address. Now, it’s as simple as opening an app like Waze or Google Maps, typing in an address and heading out. No more clumsy papers or devices that lost charge and left you stranded. Without a doubt, the internet and Wi-Fi has made local and international travel so much easier.

The way we learn

The internet is filled with more information that a single person could read in a lifetime. And, it’s all there for anyone to access. No longer do you have to go to the library to check out an encyclopaedia or journal. And more and more books can be downloaded onto a Kindle or iPad. There are such things as online courses and remote studies that make it easy to get a qualification from anywhere – as long as you have an internet connection. The options are endless.

Conclusion

The new generation might not appreciate how immediate and efficient communication has become. But it’s important to remember that this wasn’t always the way things were done. As long as we continue to interact on a personal level and make meaningful connections face-to-face, there’s nothing wrong with using advances in internet and Wi-Fi to make your life easier. This technology has changed the world for the better and the future is sure to hold exciting things.
