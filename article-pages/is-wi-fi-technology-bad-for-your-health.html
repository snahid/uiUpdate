Is Wi-Fi Technology Bad for Your Health

When it comes to Wi-Fi or cellular technology causing health problems, it is not always an easy conversation to have. There are claims that Wi-Fi causes cancer, insomnia, and even impacts children's health. Though this fear is something real that people host, the reality is that there is not much data to back it up. Fortunately, the claims lack sufficient evidence to warrant belief, but people still spread these ideas as fact, which is why this fear is still a common theme that pops up.

Wi-Fi Radiation

First and foremost, the radiation that is emitted from Wi-Fi routers is not the same kind of radiation you find in a nuclear power plant. The difference between the two is Wi-Fi radiation is non-ionizing, while the kind you find at a nuclear power plant is ionizing. Ionizing radiation breaks bonds between molecules and ionizes atoms. Examples of ionizing radiation are X-rays, cosmic rays, and the gamma rays emitted by radioactive materials. Non-Ionizing radiation does not break molecular bonds and ionize atoms. Some examples of this are radios, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and even microwaves. In summary, long wavelengths are "safe," and short wavelengths are "bad."

Microwave as An Example

Microwaves are actually what many compare Wi-Fi to because they operate on the same frequency, 2.4Ghz. The rationale goes something like, "if microwaves can heat my food, then being around Wi-Fi must be doing the same to my brain!”. This could not be further from the truth.

There are two essential components to consider in this equation; the Inverse-Square law and power output. Most microwaves operate between 700 and 900 watts, but it is not emitted outside of the device due to the heavy shielding and safe interior disbursement. The entire impact of that power output goes right to the food inside and does not escape. Compare that to a Wi-Fi router, which uses approximately 1 watt of microwave energy. While the Wi-Fi router is beaming these radio waves right through you, they will not heat you up like the food in your microwave.

What both the microwave and Wi-Fi router have in common is the Inverse-square law, which says that the intensity of light is inversely proportional to the square distance. In the case of the Wi-Fi router, the radiation exposure gets smaller the farther it travels.

Conclusion

You do not have anything to worry about when it comes to your Wi-Fi router and your health. Even though there is direct skin exposure to the Wi-Fi non-ionizing radiation, it cannot damage your DNA or cells directly. This means you will not get cancer from being around it like other, more potent ionizing radiation devices can. The World Health Organization, which is known for airing on the side of caution, says that even in high-density Wi-Fi locations like schools and hospitals, the radio-frequency radiation exposure is thousands of times lower than the international safety standards. With this information, you can have the peace of mind that you can surf the internet without causing health harm.
