Wi-Fi 6 – What Is It And What Does This Mean For 5G?

The tech world, probably more so than any other industry, is constantly changing and updating. There’s more information, more innovation and more integration taking place all the time, all over the world. Recently, we’ve been hearing more and more about the latest Wi-Fi 6 technology and how it can help to improve our already tech-savvy lives. But, as with any new developments, we have questions. What is Wi-Fi 6, and how is it going to change things in the future?

What is Wi-Fi 6?

Put very simply, Wi-Fi 6 is the next generation Wi-Fi standard that claims to be faster than all previous wireless versions. The wireless world is changing so quickly that innovators have had to come up with version numbers so engineers and consumers can keep up. Remember Wi-Fi 4 released in 2009? And then Wi-Fi 5 first launched in 2014? Well, Wi-Fi 6 is here and ready to take over.

The main goal of this new wireless technology is, obviously, to increase connection speeds, but also to help provide a better Wi-Fi performance in densely populated spaces such as stadiums, shopping malls and airports. Perhaps your Wi-Fi is even taking strain in your busy home, with kids downloading music, parents working from home and neighbours trying to get in on the action. No matter what your situation or necessity, Wi-Fi 6 is here to help.

Wi-Fi 6 vs 5G

It may not be what you think, because the two technologies are not mutually exclusive. Wi-Fi 6 hasn’t taken over 5G, but rather the two innovations complement one another very nicely. Local area networks rely on Wi-Fi, while cellular networks rely on 5G. As far as your experience as a user is concerned, both will increase performance and help you stay connected. And get things done as quickly as possible. They will both help with speed, volume and a quicker response time. Experts are saying that together, Wi-Fi 6 and 5G will “bring next-level, seamless functionality to the wireless world”.

How fast is Wi-Fi 6, really?

As mentioned, Wi-Fi 6 is designed to help with more than just speed, but it is a major component so it’s worth knowing where we stand. In technical terms, Wi-Fi 6 has a speed of 9.6 Gbps, which is 3.5 Gbps higher than Wi-Fi 5. In reality, though, both networks are incredibly quick and their speed capabilities are both maximums. A more important aspect of Wi-Fi 6 is that it helps increase network functionality when there are numerous devices connected. Rather than boosting performance on an individual basis.

Conclusion

This is our basic understanding of this new technology, but there’s more information being made available every day. In short, though, each new generation of Wi-Fi is developed to make our lives simpler and more efficient. We should start seeing Wi-Fi 6 networks being used in more and more public spaces as well as in the home. And, the transition from Wi-Fi 5 to Wi-Fi 6 technology should be fairly seamless.
