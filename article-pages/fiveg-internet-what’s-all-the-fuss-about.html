5G Internet – What’s All The Fuss About?

We’ve heard that it’s next generation, the new standard of global connectivity, a wireless technology that’s going to change the world. But there’s also been some controversy around 5G and the bigger impact it could have. After scouring the internet for as much information as possible, here’s what you need to know about this new mobile technology.

What is 5G internet?

Basically, it’s the next instalment in wireless mobile network offerings. Remember when you had 3G in the corner of your smartphone? And then the updated 4G? Well, this is said to be 10 times faster than its predecessor and substantially faster than fibre. But we’ll still keep an eye on fibre to see if there are improvements on that front, too.

According to those in the know, 5G offers speeds of around 200Mbps and has been designed to connect virtually anything – from devices to machines and objects. It’ll have a big impact in the ‘Internet of Things’ craze.

How do we use it?

Just the same way we used the previous networks. However, in South Africa, the necessary upgrades that’ll enable our smartphones and routers to take this technology might take some time. At the moment, only one phone in the country is compatible with 5G and that’s the LG V50 ThinQ. It’s also pretty pricey. You’ll also probably need to purchase a new router – rather than upgrading your existing one – and both Vodacom and Rain offer 5G-compatible routers with their packages.

What is all the controversy about?

Many say it’s unfounded and that there’s no need to worry about installing this new technology. However, if you’re prepared to go down the 5G controversy rabbit hole, you’ll find articles claiming that it’s linked to the coronavirus outbreak. In the UK, some 5G communication towers were set alight by those who thought the towers were a threat to peoples’ health.

Is there a link between 5G communication masts and COVID-19? Those who agree believe that the sickness and ailments we link to COVID-19 are really symptoms of radiation that’s emitted from the new technology’s towers. Some theorists even say there might be symptoms such as nose bleeds and long-term effects like cancer. However, industry experts and fact-checkers hold firm that these theories are ‘dangerous nonsense’. Feel free to do your own research, if you’re not convinced.

When can we expect 5G in South Africa?

Good news – it’s already here! In May 2020, Vodacom made the announcement that their 5G networks had been activated in parts of Johannesburg, Pretoria and Cape Town. But, it’s pretty costly to the consumer, coming in from around R899p/m. The other company that’s offering this new technology is Rain, and it launched their networks towards the end of 2019. With Rain, the fees are slightly higher, but they offer uncapped packages.

Conclusion

It seems as though 5G is here to stay, and more and more of us will start introducing this technology into our daily lives. It’s certainly the fastest way to connect and, in today’s white-hot society, speed is paramount. Whether you’re for it or against it, 5G is said to be the way of the future.
